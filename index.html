São Paulo, Brazil

São Paulo is the most populous and economically important state in Brazil, located in the country's Southeast region. It is home to over 46 million people and produces about one-third of Brazil's GDP. The state is known for its strong industries, finance, technology, agriculture, and international trade.
Its capital, São Paulo, is the largest city in the Southern Hemisphere and a major business and cultural hub in Latin America. In addition to its urban centers, the state features beautiful Atlantic coastline, mountains, forests, and productive farmland.
